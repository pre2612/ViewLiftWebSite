USA Today – February 12, 2015

ViewLift offers end-to-end streaming video services

The company behind online video site SnagFilms aims to snag some more of the growing streaming video business.

But instead of simply offering more content of its own, the new venture, ViewLift, provides the technological knowhow to get media companies and other firms streaming goods of their own.

Consumers have decided they like the streaming video experience. "The next battle is will I be able to watch what I want to watch, when I want to watch it and on the device I choose," said ViewLift CEO and co-founder Rick Allen.

To get their content online, content owners "need a trusted partner to help them reach that world," he said.

The ViewLift team developed its well-rounded expertise having run the ad-supported independent film site SnagFilms for three years. Over that time, it also launched several other online video services including comedy app FunnyForFree and original comedy content site Thundershorts, as well as childrens' site KiddoVid. "We have treated them as opportunities to reach consumers with a specialized portion of our content, but we have also treated them as R&D," said Allen, who is also CEO of SnagFilms.

ViewLift's various sites are available across 21 different platforms including smartphones, tablets, smart TVs, video game systems and Net video devices such as Roku. And the firm develops multiple types of delivery systems including ad-supported and subscription models.

Already, New York-based ViewLift is talking to a range of clients, Allen says, including media and education companies and other content-rich entities, as well as sports leagues and teams.

"They may have a dominant position in linear television. They may have a broad library of films and television content or they may have a content machine a sports league represents and they understand traditional distribution and traditional media but they are watching the shift in consumer behavior to stream media and to devices," he said. "They want to shorten the cycles of getting to that audience."

The company's investors include Steve Case, Ted Leonsis and Terry Semel, as well as top venture capital firms Comcast Ventures, New Enterprise Associates, Revolution LLC and Clark Enterprises.
