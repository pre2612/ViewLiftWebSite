Vice and Unilever team up for launch of female-focused channel "Broadly"

Unilever and Vice are teaming up to launch a female-focused online channel called "Broadly". The deal between Unilever and Vice will also include 11 additional online marketing channels following Broadly's launch.

"75% of Unilever's sales are to women," says Keith Weed, Unilever's chief marketing and communications officer. "We believe that Broadly can drive purposeful, authentic and lasting conversations with our consumers, particularly the worldwide community of young women who can now engage with content that focuses on what matters most to them."

This partnership is reflective of the trend for CPG brands to go beyond just selling products. Brands now want to engage, converse, and have relationship with their customers. Brands are looking to build loyal customers who value and prefer the brand over competitors.

Unilever joins the list of other brands that have developed video channels including Red Bull and Tim Horton's. ViewLift believes this is the future for CPG brands and foresee that video channels will become an integral part of the marketing portfolio.

Condé Nast Develops Brand Video Channels for Original Content to Spur Ad-revenue

Condé Nast is launching dedicated video channels for their titles such as GQ and Vogue to expand their ad-inventory. These channels will host branded short to medium-length videos.

Viewers will see ads ahead of the films they watch as well as around the player with advertisers able to link the placements with what is being show on the respective YouTube channel. Ads will not be served programmatically to the dedicated channels but Condé Nast has already started selling ads on a cost per day basis, which is primarily used for advertisers looking to maximise their share of voice around a particular campaign. Tests are also underway to look at how it monetises different sub-sets of users with ads being served on a first visit of the day or mobile visit of the day basis for example.

Condé Nast's venture into "co-creation", as this is known in the industry, demonstrates the lengths media firms to merge content with advertisement. ViewLift believes that this is an experimental and critical time for both media and brands. Media and brands who can figure out the magic formula will be in the position to captivate audiences and promote strong branding.

Maybelline-Branded Web Series ‘Vanity’ Arrives On StyleHaul YouTube Channel

In May 2015, Pemberly Digital founder Bernie Su and digital strategist David Tochterman announced Canvas Media Studios, a new venture devoted to branded content.

Just a few days later, Su announced Canvas Media Studio's first project: Vanity, a web series branded by Maybelline and available to watch on StyeHaul's Youtube channel.

Vanity stars Alex Frnka, Denise Richards and Karrueche Tran. The story revolves around a college student who attempts to learn more about the mysterious death of a famous designer by talking to her business partner. The series is mostly told through flashbacks with plenty of drama to appeal to StyleHaul's audience.

Though branded with Maybelline, the beauty brand is barely visible during the story-telling; similar to a film with dedicated or exclusive product placement. Instead, the channel adds a tasteful and surrepticious annotation at the end card where viewers can click on a featured Maybelline product.

ViewLift believes this form of branded content is the future for many brands. Creative and compelling content can not only captivate viewers, but build loyal, even fanatical, customer bases.

MCorus Entertainment Unveils First in a Suite of New Kids Apps with the Launch of TreehouseGO

As part of Corus Entertainment's expanding digital content roll out, the company is launching a full suite of TV Everywhere apps for its kids television brands starting with TreehouseGO.

TreehouseGO is the first of several apps Corus is launching to ensure audiences have convenient access to their favourite brands and content anywhere, anytime.

The focus on digital content for kids is an extremely important strategic shift. Research survey shows that kids in households with digital devices prefer tablets or mobile devices over TV. In the survey, 57% of parents say their child prefers a device other than TV to watch video content. The reasons kids prefer devices over TV include the ability to take it anywhere, easier to use, sense of independence, and ability to re-watch content over and over.

ViewLift and SnagFilms sensed the need for focused children and family friendly content, hence the creation of Kiddovid, a channel and app that curates only kids and family-friendly content.

e-Retailer Bikini.com To Launch Video Channel

Swimsuit e-tailer Bikini.com makes good on plans to become a full lifestyle brand since its acquisition by Remark Media less than two years ago.

The company debuted a video channel this year with original content covering topics such as food, travel, fashion and beauty in connection with their website editorial content.

“You see that we sell bikinis, but that’s not the focus. Our focus is really creating a lifestyle brand, creating a media channel that no one’s gone after. We think we have the best URL to do that and we can scale globally because in every language a bikini’s a bikini," says Kai-Shing Tao, CEO and Chairman of Remark.

ViewLift strongly believes that retailers must incorporate video content as part of their content marketing, and overall marketing, portfolio. Web-based editorial content has already reached maturity and limitations.

The consumer behavior has already shifted towards more mobile usage and retailers are aware of this shift, hence the retailer "doctrine" of omnichannel. Furthermore, consumers have demonstrated that they respond positively to video content in surveys and research. In order for retailers to maintain a competitive edge, they must dare to incorporate branded video marketing.
